Career

Virat Kohli is one of the most famous cricketers in the world and a legendary player of the India national cricket team. He made his international cricket debut in One Day Internationals (ODIs) in 2008 against the Sri Lanka national cricket team. The match was played on 18 August 2008 in Dambulla, Sri Lanka. At that time, Kohli was just 19 years old and had recently led the Indian team to victory in the 2008 Under-19 Cricket World Cup. Because of his excellent performance in youth cricket, he was selected to play for the senior Indian team. In his debut match, Kohli opened the batting for India. Although he scored only 12 runs from 22 balls, it was an important moment in his life because it marked the beginning of his international cricket career. Even though his score was small, he showed confidence and good batting technique. Playing at the international level helped him gain valuable experience and learn from senior players in the team. Over time, Kohli worked very hard to improve his batting skills and fitness. His dedication and determination helped him become one of the best batsmen in the world. After his ODI debut, he later made his Test debut in 2011 and also became an important player in T20 cricket. Kohli became famous for his aggressive batting style, consistency, and ability to chase big targets. Because of his outstanding performances, he was later appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team. Under his leadership, India achieved many memorable victories. He has scored many centuries and broken several records in international cricket. Kohli is admired for his passion, discipline, and strong work ethic. Many young cricketers look up to him as a role model and inspiration. From scoring just 12 runs in his debut match to becoming one of the greatest batsmen in cricket history, Kohli’s journey is truly inspiring. His debut in 2008 was the first step in a remarkable career that continues to inspire millions of cricket fans around the world.

Highlights

Virat Kohli is one of the greatest cricketers in modern cricket and an important player of the India national cricket team. He was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Kohli developed an interest in cricket at a very young age and started playing professionally during his school days. His talent and dedication quickly made him stand out among other young players. One of the biggest highlights of his early career was leading the Indian team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. This success brought him into the spotlight and helped him earn a place in the senior Indian team. Kohli made his One Day International debut in 2008 against the Sri Lanka national cricket team in Dambulla. Although he scored only 12 runs in his first match, it marked the beginning of a remarkable international career. Over the years, Kohli worked very hard to improve his batting technique and physical fitness. His determination and passion for cricket helped him become one of the most consistent batsmen in the world. He scored his first ODI century in 2009 and soon became a regular member of the Indian team. Kohli made his Test debut in 2011 against the West Indies national cricket team and gradually proved his ability in the longest format of the game. In the same year, he was part of the Indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, which was a proud moment for the country. Kohli is known for his aggressive batting style, strong determination, and excellent chasing ability in limited-overs cricket. Because of his outstanding performances, he later became the captain of the Indian cricket team in all three formats. Under his leadership, India achieved many memorable victories and became the number one Test team in the world for several years. One of the historic achievements during his captaincy was India's Test series victory in Australia in 2018–19. Kohli has scored more than 70 international centuries and thousands of runs across all formats of the game. He is also considered one of the fastest batsmen to reach several milestones in ODI cricket. His strong work ethic and focus on fitness changed the fitness culture of the Indian cricket team. Kohli has received many prestigious awards for his achievements, including the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna and the Padma Shri from the Government of India. Apart from his cricket career, Kohli is also known for his charity work and support for young athletes. Millions of fans around the world admire him for his dedication, confidence, and passion for the game. Many young cricketers consider him a role model and inspiration. From a young boy playing cricket in Delhi to becoming one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport, Virat Kohli’s journey is truly inspiring. His achievements, leadership, and consistent performances have made him a legend in international cricket and one of the most respected sports personalities in the world.

Marriage life

Virat Kohli has a happy marriage and family life. He married famous Indian actress Anushka Sharma on 11 December 2017 in a private ceremony in Tuscany. Their wedding was one of the most talked-about celebrity weddings in India. Virat Kohli and Anushka Sharma are often called one of the most popular and loved couples in the country. They support each other’s careers and are often seen together at events and cricket matches. In January 2021, the couple welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika Kohli. Later, in February 2024, they were blessed with a son named Akaay Kohli. Kohli is known to be a caring husband and a loving father who values spending time with his family despite his busy cricket schedule. He often shares that his family gives him strength, happiness, and motivation in life. The couple also focuses on maintaining a private and peaceful life for their children while balancing their successful careers. Virat Kohli’s strong bond with his wife and children shows the importance he gives to family along with his achievements in cricket.
